Spontaneous Fermenation VS Starter Cultures

Stages of Microbial Transformation

  • Stage 1: Leuconostoc mesenteroides
    • Produces carbon dioxide
    • Replaces oxygen
    • Acid sensitive
  • Lactobacillus plantarum
    • Continues to consume sugars
    • Homofermentative
  • Lactobacillus brevis
    • Continues to lower pH
    • Once finished, no more bubbles

chef's knife

Basic Knife Skills

  • Knife grip
  • Finger curl
  • Thumb push

How to make sauerkraut

Prep Cabbage

  • Remove and save outer leaves
  • Slice cabbage into quarters
  • Save the core
  • Shred the cabbage
    • Thin
    • Thick
    • Squares

Choose Your Salt

Types of Salt

  • Table Salt
    • Iodine
    • Anti-Caking Agents
  • Kosher Salt
    • No iodine
    • Sometimes anti-caking
  • Sea Salt
    • Refined or unrefined
    • Sometimes iodized
  • Pickling Salt
    • Highly refined

Measuring Salt

  • Volumentric measurements are not accurate
  • Weigh your salt
  • Or keep batches small

Salt Percentage

  • 2 to 3%
  • More and bacteria can't thrive
  • Less and might develop slimy or off flavors

Salt and Massage Cabbage

  • Break down cellular walls
  • Osmosis
  • Juice, Juice, Juic
  • Water is plain

Pack cabbage

Keep Below the Brine

  • Finger method
  • Weights
    • Glass weights
    • Clay weights
    • Rocks
  • Jar-inside-jar method
    • Cover with cloth
    • Or close inside jar lid
  • Plastic bag

Release CO2

  • Check for first week
  • May overflow
  • Mild danger of pressure buildup

Fermentation Length

  • 3 days to 6 months
  • Colder = slower
  • Warmer = faster

pH Drop

  • 4.6 or lower
  • High acid food
  • Resists spoilage

Refrigerate when ready

Alternative Flavors

  • Juniper Berry & Caraway Seeds
  • Spicy Sauerkraut
  • Brussels Sprouts Sauerkraut
